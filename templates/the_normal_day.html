Start of the Day

When students come in they will take the chairs at their seats down.

Start of Class

The Greeting

  • I will be standing in front of my door, ready to great you with a smile and saying "Welcome Back! Go ahead to your desk and get started on your Brain Sweat activity." I only do this because you may be having an off day and you just need a little bit of reminding on the procedures.
  • If there was any homework the night before, I will also remind you to go ahead and put it on your desk so that way my collector can go around and pick up your homework and place it inside of the "I Did Stuff at Home" tray.
  • If you had to get something signed (field trip form, parent notes, etc.) you can put it in the "Nitty Gritty" tray and I'll remind you of that too as you walk in. This will be at the corner of my desk. Next to this will be some envelopes and I will kindly ask you to put your forms or field trip money in there with your name on top of it please. I would hate to either lose something, or someone getting into your privacy.

    • Your inside: Now What!?

  • Every day there will be a different genre of music playing inside of the classroom. I will always try my best to keep the music upbeat. I do this because, again, I want my classroom to have a positive feel inside of it.
  • When you are in the room, if you are allowed to have a backpack, go ahead and take out your need materials for the day and place them under your desk or chair. I would hate for someone to trip over your backpack or for your backpack to break from someone stepping on it.
  • If you have a coat on you can hang your coat on the back of your chair or ask permission to put it in your locker for the same reasoning as it is for the backpacks.
  • When you have your materials out, coat on the back of the chair, backpack underneath your desk or chair, you can get started on the Brain Sweat/warm up activity activity.

    • Attendance

    There will be flash cards with your name facing up, when you enter the classroom you you will flip the card over that will say your name then here underneath of it.
    What Will the Teach be Doing While Your Brain Sweats?
  • I will be doing some administration stuff for the office
  • Collecting the envelopes that are inside my "Nitty Gritty" tray.
  • Collect the Homework out of folders
    • After Brain Sweat
  • I will go over that days Brain Sweat with you so you know how to do it and will allow for any questions you may have about it. I don't want to leave any student confused about what is going on.
  • When we go over it I will ask a student to talk about or maybe even demonstrate they got their answer or what the answer is, but when you do this I will have you stand up. I want each of my wonderful students to be comfortable speaking in front of people and for each of my students to respect a speaker by listening quietly.
  • Once we go over the Brain Sweat I will talk to you about what we are doing that day and provide you with the needed materials for the day. I will brief you on it so that way you know what is going on that day instead of staring at me with blank eyes thinking "whaaaaa?"
  • While you are on task and working on your assignment I will be walking around to answer any questions, listen to conversations, and pushing you to take your conversations even deeper.
    • Ending the Class
  • At the last 5 or ten minutes of class I will assign any homework if there is any.
  • I will also have an exit slip prepared for you. I will not let you leave until you have finished the exit slip. I do this because I want to make sure you understood what we did that day. I would hate for my brilliant student to fall behiind!
  • You may be wondering, what about my assignment I just did! How do I turn that in? Well there will be a job for that too! The collector will pick up the papers and put them in the "I Did Stuff in Class" tray.
  • During this time the janitor should be going through and cleaning up desk areas, and sweeping where it is messy. After all a clean environment is a happy one!
    • Ending the Day
  • You will put the chairs up on your desk once you finish and turn in the exit slip.
  • I will be standing in front of the door, waiting for the final bell and I will let you out once the room is an the appropriate condition.
