How the Pay Scale Works

  • Each jobs pay will have to do with how important the job is. A banker will get paid more than most because that job holds much responsibility. A janitor will also get paid more because cleanliness is highly important.
  • Your pay will be bi-weekly, meaning you will get paid every two weeks.
  • Tax will be deducted from your paycheck. The classroom tax will be 5% of what you earned. To find how much you will REALLY make every two weeks here is an example of how to do it.
    100*0.05 = 95. So take the amount you will make (100) times the tax amount (5% = 0.05).

    • Janitor

    Pay

  • As a Janitor you will make about $650 per week before taxes. This group gets paid more because when I say clean I mean CLEAN. I expect the sink to be cleaned, cabinets wiped down, and floor swept.

    • Job Description

  • You will be responsible for cleaning the room that class period so that way when the next class comes in the room is not messy, possibly causing someone to trip and hurt themselves. You will also be providing a clean environment, which in turns helps create a positive one.

    • Skills Needed

  • Hard working. You need time to clean, so you need to finish classwork to do your job.
  • Creative. We may have a full class period of work, you need to find time to do your job. If you are walking near a sink, clean it then.

    • How Many Are There?

  • There will be about 3 to 5 students in each class.

    • How to Apply

    To apply I need you to give me four things:
    1. An application
    2. A cover letter
    3. A resume
    4. A letter of recommendation from someone who would know you cleanliness. (ex: parent)
