The Truth

  • I will not lie to you my students, I will have high expectations for you. I do not do this to make your life hard, or because I want to see a student so stressed that they break down. I have been there before, it is not fun. I do this because I know you are capable of great things.
  • Although my expectations will be high, I will make you think, none of my tasks will be "impossible."

    • Classwork

  • I expect you to try your hardest on your classwork. I am in there for you, you have table mates who are willing to help you. Use us, we want you to succeed. If you feel like you are giving up, tell me. I know what it feels like to get defeated over and over, sometimes you need to step away from the problem then go for it again. Point I am trying to make is this:
    Don't give me less than your very best.
  • During your classwork I expect there to be conversation, deep discussions. But I want those discussions to be on topic, I want to see how much thought you can give into the assignment.

    • Homework

  • You will normally get one or two assignments a week. I know homework is not fun, but I will use it to benefit you, as a student. I want to make sure you understand what is going on in class. If you feel lost, feel free to email me or call me. I will try my best to explain what is going on in your assignment.
  • I will normally give you at least 2 to 3 days to complete the assignment, so that way if you are lost you have time to ask any questions you may have.

    • Classroom Expectations

  • Inside of the classroom I want each of my wonderful students to feel respected. Not just by me, but by each other. So that means I do not want to see any signs of bullying, but I know I won't have that problem because each of you are wonderful, caring people.
  • When someone speaks, listen. You would want someone to do the same for you, so do that for the speaker. It can be very unnerving to speak in front of people for some people.
  • If someone seems upset, talk to them. They may just need a friend. Spread a positive attitude, after all a smile is contagious.
